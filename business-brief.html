Pricing & margins

Revised pricing across all three kits

The £300 upfront / £20pm subscription on the 400W is feasible — but tight at early-stage COGS of ~£175. Your gross margin drops to ~43% vs the original ~55%. That's still commercially viable; the tradeoff is you need slightly higher volume to cover fixed overheads. At scale (200+ unit orders), COGS drops toward £140 and margin recovers to ~53%.
KitUpfront priceSub priceSub total (18mo)Landed COGSGross profit (upfront)Gross margin
Sola 200 Starter £189 £13/mo £234 £100–120 £69–89 37–47%
Sola 400 Hero ⭐ £300 £20/mo £360 £155–175 £125–145 42–48%
Sola 800 Pro £549 £35/mo £630 £240–280 £269–309 49–56%
On the ROI framing at £20/month: The Hero 400W saves £10–13/month on bills. Net monthly cost to consumer is £7–10. This is a better pitch than £29/month (was £16–19 net), but still not cash-positive from month 1. The honest framing remains: "spread the cost, own it outright after 18 months, then save forever." At £300 upfront, the payback is a genuinely strong 2.0–2.3 years — lead with that for upfront buyers.
The hard truth about month-1 cash-positivity: To be genuinely net-positive from month 1, the monthly payment would need to be below ~£10. At that level you collect only £180 over 18 months — below your COGS. There is no pricing at which this product is cash-positive from month 1 without either subsidising it (a loss-leader) or waiting for COGS to fall further at scale. The right move: don't promise month-1 ROI. Instead, say "lowest monthly cost of any kit on the market — own it outright in 18 months, saving for 10+ years after."

Total addressable market

Who can actually use Sola?

The UK market is much larger than just balcony owners. The full TAM includes renters with gardens, homeowners wanting a portable/supplemental system, and even boat/caravan owners. Here's the breakdown.
Private renter households
4.6M+
England alone. Growing every year as house prices keep people renting longer. Primary target — can't get rooftop solar, can get Sola.
UK homes with gardens
~21M
87% of UK's ~24M households have garden access. Includes renters and owners. Garden installs need no balcony — Sola 800 on a ground frame works for all of these.
UK flats (leasehold)
~4.98M
70% of all leasehold homes are flats. Many have balconies or accessible outdoor space. These are your highest-concentration urban customers.

Shipping & delivery

Shipping costs by kit

Panel dimensions and weight vary significantly — the 800W Pro ships as two large panels (172cm × 113cm × 3.5cm, 21.8kg each) and may require a pallet or oversized courier. The 200 and 400 compact panels are standard parcel territory. Your 3PL handles this automatically but you need to price delivery accurately on Shopify.
KitPackage dims (est.)Total weightStandard delivery (3–5 days)Next daySuggested charge to customerYour margin on delivery
Sola 200 90×65×12cm ~8kg £7–10 £12–16 Free standard · £9.99 next day £0 / ~£4 margin
Sola 400 ⭐ 90×65×20cm ~16kg £9–13 £15–20 Free standard · £12.99 next day £0 / ~£4 margin
Sola 800 180×120×15cm ×2 ~50kg £25–40 £45–65 £14.99 standard · £34.99 next day −£5 to +£5
Strategy for 200 and 400: Offer free standard delivery — this removes friction and is the norm for electronics. Build the £9–13 shipping cost into your COGS/pricing. Charge for next day (£10–13 to customer, costs you £13–18, so small subsidy or break-even). Most customers choose free standard.
Strategy for 800: Two rigid 172cm panels make this an oversized/heavy shipment — you can't absorb it. Charge £14.99 standard (costs you ~£25–35 via 3PL with business rates, so a subsidy of ~£10–20). Honest option: offer "free delivery when you subscribe" to incentivise the subscription model, where your margin is higher anyway.
Important: The 800W Pro panels (172cm × 113cm each) may exceed standard courier limits. DHL handles up to 120cm × 70cm × 70cm. At 172cm length, these may require Parcelforce 48 (up to 1.5m length) or a specialist freight carrier. Your 3PL will handle routing, but flag this when onboarding them — ask specifically about "long/large" handling and fragile electronics surcharges. Budget £30–45 per 800W delivery at first.

Packaging strategy

On-box vs. leaflet instructions

Recommendation: hybrid approach. Print the 5-step install guide on the box (as shown in the packaging designs), but also include one thin A5 folded card inside with:

1) The landlord permission template letter (pre-written, consumer just signs)
2) The Sola app QR code and Wi-Fi pairing instructions
3) Warranty registration card
Why on-box instructions win: Customers frequently throw away instruction leaflets before reading them. If the 5 steps are on the box itself, the instructions are always present — before, during, and after unboxing. This is what Apple does with AirPods; the packaging is the instruction manual. It also signals quality and confidence in the product's simplicity.
📦

Unboxing experience strategy

The box opens from the top. Panel(s) visible immediately in protective foam. Inverter and cable in a branded tray underneath. One card on top with just three words: "Plug in. Generate. Save." Everything else is on the box exterior.

♻️

Sustainable packaging signal

Use recycled card (FSC certified). Print "100% recycled packaging" on the box — this resonates with your eco audience and costs ~£1–2 extra per unit. The box should be strong enough to reuse for storage — don't make it feel disposable.

🎁

Gift-worthy design

Father's Day and Christmas require a gift-ready box. The amber/teal/dark colourway already looks premium. Add "Gift wrapping available" at checkout (£3.99 extra — free money, zero effort for 3PL).
