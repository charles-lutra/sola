Brand identity

Who is Sola?

Brand positioning in one sentence: "The solar kit for people who rent, move, and want their money back from the energy companies."

Brand personality
If Sola were a person, they'd be… A 31-year-old renter in Bristol who cycles to work, tracks their bills on a spreadsheet, and spent an hour researching every appliance they own to make it more efficient. They're practical, not preachy. They care about the planet but they'll talk about £ savings first, because that's what actually convinces people.

Tone words: Direct. Warm. A little wry. Smart without showing off. Never preachy.
Two audiences, one brand voice
Eco-conscious renters (25–38) Lead with energy sovereignty, climate context, renter rights. Conversational, slightly urgent. They want to feel like they're doing something.

Cost-focused households (38–60) Lead with £ numbers, payback period, "saves more than it costs." More formal. They want proof, not ideology. Reference the Ofgem price cap, energy crisis.

Both audiences respond to: simplicity, portability, no-jargon explanations.

Social media

Email marketing

Post-purchase email flow

Day 0 — Order confirmation
Your Sola 400 is on its way ☀️
Order details + tracking. Quick "what to expect" — arrives in 3–5 days, everything you need is in the box. Link to installation video. Build excitement.
Day 3 — Pre-install
Before you unbox: one thing to do first
Check your balcony direction — use Google Maps or the Sola app. South or south-west = optimal. Eastward is fine. Include the landlord template letter if they need it. Manage expectations warmly.
Day 7 — First week savings
How much did your solar save this week?
Prompt to open the app and check. Show them how to read their generation data. Include the maths: "at this rate, you'll save £X this year." Reinforce the purchase decision. Share on social CTA.
Day 30 — Review request
One month of free electricity — what do you think?
Ask for a Trustpilot / Google review. Show their personal month-1 savings from app data. Introduce the referral programme: "give a friend £25 off, get £25 credit." Soft upsell: battery add-on for evening savings.
Month 3 — Battery upsell
Your panels generated X kWh last month. Here's how to save even more.
Introduce the Sola Battery add-on. Show the maths: without battery = 40% self-consumption. With battery = 75%. "If you're out during the day, you're losing half your solar generation." Offer 10% loyalty discount.
Month 6 — Price cap / news moment
Bills going up again. Yours aren't (as much).
Triggered by price cap announcement. Remind them their solar means this hurts less. Referral push: "your neighbours are about to get hit. Help them." Builds loyalty and drives word-of-mouth at perfect moment.
Tools & automation

The lean marketing stack

Content scheduling
Buffer (free–£12/mo) or Later (£16/mo). Schedule a month of posts in one 2-hour session. TikTok requires native upload for full reach — schedule the draft, post natively. Instagram can auto-post via API.
Email marketing
Klaviyo (free to 250 contacts, then ~£20/mo). Shopify-native integration. Set up all flows above once — they run forever. Segment by: purchase tier, days since install, app engagement.
AI content generation
Use Claude to write a full month of captions, scripts, and email copy in one session. Feed in energy news headlines and ask for reactive posts. Build a "brand voice prompt" once — paste it at the start of every session to ensure consistency.
Customer support
Tidio (£19/mo) or Gorgias (Shopify-native, £10/mo). AI chatbot handles 60–70% of queries. Ticket system for the rest. Use Claude to draft your FAQ knowledge base content.
SEO content
Target: "can I install solar panels in rented house UK," "plug in solar panels UK," "balcony solar UK," "save money on energy bills UK." Write one long-form guide per month. These rank fast in a new market with little competition.
PR moments
Set a Google Alert for: "energy price cap," "Ofgem," "plug-in solar UK," "balcony solar." Pre-write reactive templates for each scenario. Submit to MoneySavingExpert forum, Which? reader stories, local BBC features. Zero-cost earned media.
